# What could be happening?
## 1) The setting itself overpowers incentives
### Primary care and Urgent care settings could be causing the preverse behavior -- so not just MD vs. NP vs. PA, but maybe more so MDs in this setting act more preversly than MDs in higher acuity, less discretionary, settings. 

## 2) PAs are really the ones taking advantage
### They get less attention from MDs potentially than NPs, since they are a higher credential, yet basically have the same incentives as NPs -- they also seem to get less attention from lawmakers and the public.
