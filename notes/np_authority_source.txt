1. The Source for NP Scope of Practice (Authority)
The gold standard source used universally in health economics and policy literature (e.g., Alexander and Schnell 2019, Traczynski and Udalova 2018) is the American Association of Nurse Practitioners (AANP).

They officially maintain the State Practice Environment map and categorize states strictly into:

Full Practice: NPs can evaluate, diagnose, order tests, and prescribe medications independently.

Reduced Practice: NPs have independent practice for at least one element, but require a collaborative agreement with a physician for others (usually prescribing).

Restricted Practice: NPs are required by law to have career-long supervision, delegation, or team management by a physician in order to provide any patient care.

Citation to keep in your notes: American Association of Nurse Practitioners. "State Practice Environment." AANP.org.
